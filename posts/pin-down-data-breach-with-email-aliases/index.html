Pin down data breach with email aliases

Dec 12 2020

A long time ago I stumbled upon interesting gmail feature - trivial way to create email aliases by using “+” character support.google.com/a/users/answer/9308648 . Since then, every time I needed to sign in somewhere, I used account-specific email address. For example my.email+imdb@example.com.

Years passed and I didn’t notice any outcome of this approach. I even started questioning value of this practice. But just recently I observed something unusual. Higher number of spam emails. Quick inspection revealed that all of them were sent to the only one of my email aliases. That excited me, I detected data breach and I know who is the culprit! I may be the first to know! Quickly checked at monitor.firefox.com to confirm that and in this case it was already publicly known. Nevertheless, what happened was exactly why I came up with account-specific email address approach in the first place. I wanted to be able to associate data leak with its source. There is one loophole though. Not all websites accept emails like my.email+imdb@example.com, which, by the way, is example of needlessly strict validation.
